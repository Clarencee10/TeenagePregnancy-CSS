Resources for Pregnant Teenagers

1. Healthcare Services

Accessing proper healthcare during pregnancy is essential for the well-being of both the teenager and her baby. In the Philippines, pregnant teenagers can avail themselves of the following services:

  • Local health centers and barangay health stations offering prenatal care services
  • Government hospitals and public health facilities providing maternal and child health services, including free or low-cost prenatal check-ups
  • PhilHealth, the national health insurance program, which covers maternity benefits for qualified individuals

Additionally, some private hospitals and clinics may offer specialized adolescent maternal care services.

2. Counseling and Support

Pregnancy can be emotionally challenging for teenagers. These resources offer counseling and support:

  • Teenage Pregnancy Hotline (163) operated by the Department of Health (DOH), offering counseling, information, and referrals to support services
  • Community-based organizations and NGOs such as Likhaan Center for Women's Health, providing counseling and reproductive health services for teenagers
  • Adolescent-friendly health facilities endorsed by the DOH, which offer confidential counseling and support services for pregnant teenagers

Many schools in the Philippines also have guidance counselors or peer counseling programs that can provide support to pregnant students.

3. Educational and Vocational Assistance

Continuing education and career development are important for pregnant teenagers' future:

  • Alternative Learning System (ALS) offered by the Department of Education (DepEd), providing non-formal education programs such as the Accreditation and Equivalency (A&E) Test for out-of-school youth, including pregnant teenagers
  • Technical Education and Skills Development Authority (TESDA) programs offering skills training and vocational courses, including those tailored for young parents
  • Government scholarships and financial assistance programs such as the Expanded Students' Grants-in-Aid Program for Poverty Alleviation (ESGPPA) and the Tertiary Education Subsidy (TES)

Some non-profit organizations may also offer scholarship opportunities specifically for pregnant and parenting students.

4. Parenting Classes and Resources

Preparing for parenthood involves learning practical skills and accessing resources:

  • Community health centers and barangay health stations offering prenatal and parenting classes, including information on newborn care, breastfeeding, and family planning
  • Government initiatives such as the "Buntis Congress" organized by local government units, providing prenatal education and support to expectant mothers
  • NGOs like the Philippine Red Cross offering first aid and childcare training programs
  • Local support groups and church-based organizations providing assistance with baby essentials and childcare resources

5. National Campaign to Prevent Teen and Unplanned Pregnancy:

  • This organization focuses on raising awareness about the consequences of teen and unplanned pregnancies through educational resources, campaigns, and advocacy efforts. Their goal is to empower young people with the information and resources they need to make informed decisions about their sexual health and prevent unintended pregnancies.

6. National Organization on Adolescent Pregnancy, Parenting and Prevention (NOAPPP)

  • NOAPPP provides a range of resources, training, and support for professionals working with pregnant and parenting adolescents. They offer programs aimed at empowering young parents to continue their education, access healthcare, and develop parenting skills, as well as advocating for policies that support adolescent parents.

7. Healthy Start

  • Healthy Start is a federally funded program focused on reducing infant mortality and improving maternal and child health outcomes in high-risk communities. While not exclusively focused on teenage pregnancy, Healthy Start often provides support and resources for pregnant teenagers and young parents, including prenatal care, parenting classes, and assistance accessing social services.

8. March of Dimes

  • The March of Dimes is a nonprofit organization dedicated to improving the health of mothers and babies. They offer resources and support for pregnant teenagers, including information on prenatal care, childbirth education, and healthy behaviors during pregnancy. Additionally, they fund research to address issues related to preterm birth and birth defects.

9. Children's Bureau

  • As part of the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, the Children's Bureau provides resources, support, and information on parenting and child welfare. They offer services for teenage parents and their families, including parenting education, case management, and assistance accessing social services like childcare and housing support.

10. The Adolescent Pregnancy Prevention Campaign of North Carolina (APPCNC)

  • This organization focuses on preventing teenage pregnancy and supporting young parents in North Carolina through resources, advocacy, and support programs. They offer parenting classes, educational assistance, and advocacy efforts aimed at addressing the root causes of teenage pregnancy and promoting healthy relationships and decision-making among young people.

Additionally, some hospitals and birthing facilities in the Philippines offer maternity packages that include prenatal classes and postnatal support services.
